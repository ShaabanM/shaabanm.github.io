About

I am currently a graduate student at the University of Toronto Department of Physics and the Dunlap Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics. Before that, I recieved my BSc (Hons) in Physics and Mathematics from the University of British Columbia.

My primary research interests are cosmology and instrumentation. I am particularly interested in experimentally probing the time evolution of dark energy. I am currently working with the Balloon Astrophysics Group under the supervision of Dr. Barth Netterfield. We are developing a balloon borne weak lensing telescope.

My CV can be found here: ShaabanCV. If you are interested in learning more about my research projects there are both a public accessible section and a detailed section under the research tab. If you are an undergraduate student I point you to the teaching and outreach tab, there you can find some potentially helpful resources.

Short Bio

I have also had the privelage to visit over 130 cities in over 30 countries trying at least one ice-cream store per city.
